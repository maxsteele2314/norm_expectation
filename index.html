Cairo: Norm vs. Expectation

The process of visually exploring data is summarized by Cairo: find patterns and trends lurking in the data and then observe the deviations from those patterns. A norm is a model of what should exist or be followed or is the average model that is selcted in a given context. An exception is an unplanned event that occurs while a program is executing and disrupts the flow of its instructions. Both of these terms refer to the visually displayed aspects of a given data set. Whether that is how the numbers are displayed, the text is displayed or even what type of chart is selected.
